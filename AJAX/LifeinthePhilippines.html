Life in the Philippines

The Philippines is a small country located in Southeast Asia. Being that we were colonized by the Spaniards for 300 years and the Americans for about 50 years, I guess we can say that our culture is a mix of the West and Asia combined. With its two major languages as Filipino and English, quite a few Filipinos are bilingual. This doesn't count the fact that the Philippines also has plenty of dialects (Tagalog is its main one) which means that there are Filipinos that can speak Tagalog, English, and another Filipino dialect too.

An interesting fact about the Philippines is that it is a third-world country. A misconception that I would like to correct though is that the term "third-world country" does not automatically mean that the country has no running water or electricity. The term "third-world" country only means that it is a developing country and this is probably used to describe the Philippines as we are much behind with things such as technology development and the like. Also, many Filipinos are below the poverty line which is probably another factor.

With over 7,000 islands, the Philippines is divided into three main "lands" or pulo as we call it: Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. Luzon is the biggest pulo in the Philippines and is also where the capital, Manila (also where I grew up), is located.

The main religion of the Philippines is Catholicism. In Mindanao, the dominant religion is Muslim which causes some problems in the country (a small civil war).

Being a tropical country rich with resources, the main industries of the Philippines are farming and fishing. Agriculture in the Philippines though is very different from agriculture in America. Usually, we grow things such as pineapples, rice (our staple food), sugar canes etc.
